IPS Database Project

Team Origins Development and the Database Project

The members of team Origins Development are Calvin Edwards, James Hufnagel and Jane Tae. They are the second-year studnets who are studying Information and Computer Systems at Camosun College.

Our client is the Indigenous Perspectives Society has experienced difficulty with their current database system. Their current system is data-driven and neglects the user's experience(UX). The design is intimidating to existing and new users; without an overview for employee tasks relating to data records, finding the correct information can be a daunting experience.
Their current database’s features are underutilized and create a risk of creating deficiencies in the workflow. The proposed project intends to give an overview of all the tasks that are required for government and charity organization compliance standards.










The Organization





The client is the Indigenous Perspectives Society; the society is a charitable organization that supports the First Nation communities and culture across British Columbia. The development team meets with some leading members of the IPS:

  • Elaine Zamardi, Executive and Project Coordinator
  • Candace McKivett, Program Development Project Lead
  • Linda Lucas, Special Projects Coordinator
  • Rachelle Dallaire, Executive Director

Through project demonstrations, the Origins Development team has built a trusting rapport with the IPS. Open communication and flexible meetings are foundational tenants between the development team and the project sponsor contacts.

The Project

The Origins Development team has made significant progress on the Indigenous Perspectives Society (IPS) a Database Project; the database, API layer and the front-end browser client are almost reaching their expected levels of progress.

To give ample time for the IPS to fully define the functionality required, the development team has focused on modular components without heavily investing in undefined behaviour based on assumptions of IPS user needs; however, this has prevented us from entering a testing state. The database has been fully normalized to reflect the business logic of the IPS as per the project charter’s scope. The front-end browser client has structure and styling for various widgets to allow users to view data in multiple ways.

We are in the process of fully developing theses feature, we are on track to finish more than the project’s defined scope by August 16th.
